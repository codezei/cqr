Reactive Services

Continuous Monitoring

  • 24/7 Real-Time Surveillance: Our MSOC provides constant, around-the-clock monitoring of your OT systems to detect and respond to threats immediately.
  • Secure Connectivity: Utilizing VPN connections and client-side listeners installed at your site, we ensure seamless and secure system oversight without compromising your operations.

Threat Detection and Response

  • Rapid Alerts and Notifications: We guarantee a5-minute response time to notify you of any detected security alerts or anomalies.
  • Dedicated OT Security Experts: Our team of specialists is on standby 24/7 to respond to and mitigate threats promptly.
  • Incident Reporting: Detailed incident reports are generated and sent to you, providing comprehensive insights into any security events.

Advanced Analytics

  • AI-Powered Threat Detection: Leveraging artificial intelligence and machine learning, we proactively hunt for threats, identifying potential vulnerabilities before they can be exploited.
  • Customized OT Threat Intelligence: We deliver tailored threat intelligence feeds specific to your industrial control systems, enhancing detection accuracy.
  • Regular Reporting and Trend Analysis: Receive detailed insights and trend analyses to continuously improve your security posture over time.

Risk Assessment

  • Identify Potential Threats: Conduct comprehensive evaluations to uncover vulnerabilities in both IT and OT systems.
  • Assess Risk Levels: Determine the severity and likelihood of potential risks to prioritize mitigation efforts.
  • Develop Mitigation Strategies: Create proactive plans to address and reduce identified risks.

Compliance Assessment

  • Analyze Regulatory Compliance: Review current practices against industry standards and regulatory requirements.
  • Identify Compliance Gaps: Detect areas where compliance is lacking or needs improvement.
  • Recommend Corrective Actions: Suggest measures to achieve full compliance and avoid potential penalties.

Maturity Assessment

  • Evaluate Cybersecurity Maturity: Assess the current maturity levels of your organization's people, processes, and technologies.
  • Identify Improvement Areas: Highlight gaps and areas needing enhancement.
  • Develop Improvement Roadmap: Create a strategic plan to elevate your cybersecurity posture over time.

Architecture Review

  • Secure Network Architecture: Examine the design and structure of your network systems for security weaknesses.
  • Analyze Connections: Assess both physical and logical connections within your network.
  • Recommend Enhancements: Provide suggestions to strengthen network security and resilience.

OT Cybersecurity Strategy and Program Development

  • Develop Security Policies: Establish comprehensive policies tailored to your OT environment.
  • Implement Procedures: Define procedures that support and enforce these security policies.
  • Deploy Cybersecurity Framework: Introduce a robust framework to guide ongoing security efforts.

Awareness and Training Programs

  • Specialized Staff Training: E Conduct training sessions for staff at all levels to promote a security-conscious culture.
  • Develop Awareness Materials: Create resources to increase cybersecurity awareness among employees.
  • Measure Effectiveness: Assess the impact of training programs to ensure objectives are met.

Threat Hunting

  • Simulated Incident Preparation: Design realistic scenarios to test incident response capabilities.
  • Facilitate Exercises: Lead practice sessions to improve readiness for actual incidents.
  • Evaluate Performance: Provide feedback and recommendations for enhancement.

Vulnerability Assessment

  • Identify Hidden Threats: Conduct regular threat hunts to uncover undetected vulnerabilities.
  • Mitigate Risks: Actively search for and address potential threats before they become incidents.
  • Report Findings: Document findings and actions taken to inform future strategies.

Tabletop Exercises

  • Comprehensive Scanning: Perform detailed scans to identify vulnerabilities in systems and networks.
  • Risk Prioritization: Rank vulnerabilities based on their risk level to prioritize remediation efforts.
  • Actionable Recommendations: Provide guidance on addressing identified vulnerabilities effectively.

Incident Response

  • Objective: Immediate Response to Incidents
  • 24/7 Incident Response Coordination: We organize and manage response efforts around the clock to minimize the impact of cybersecurity incidents.
  • Rapid Onsite and Offsite Support: Our team provides immediate assistance both remotely and on-site to contain and mitigate threats swiftly.
  • Tailored Incident Response Strategy: We develop customized response plans specific to your organization's needs and infrastructure.

Forensic Analysis

  • Objective: Investigate and Analyze Incidents
  • In-Depth Investigation: We determine the cause and full impact of security breaches through comprehensive analysis.
  • Detailed Incident Reporting: Findings are thoroughly documented to inform stakeholders and guide future prevention efforts.
  • Root Cause Analysis: We identify exploited vulnerabilities and recommend improvements to prevent recurrence.

Remediation Planning

  • Objective: Recover and Restore Operations
  • Develop Remediation Plans: We create actionable strategies to recover systems and restore normal operations promptly.
  • Collaborate with Internal Teams: Working closely with your staff ensures effective implementation of remediation efforts.

Disaster Recovery Planning

  • Objective: Ensure Business Continuity After Incidents
  • Business Continuity Strategies: We help develop plans to maintain essential functions during and after a crisis.
  • System Recovery Protocols: Establish procedures for data recovery and system restoration to minimize downtime.
  • Testing and Validation: Conduct drills and simulations to ensure the effectiveness of disaster recovery plans.

Disaster Recovery Planning

  • Objective: Improve Future Response and Prevention
  • Conduct Thorough Analyses: We examine incident responses to identify strengths and areas for improvement.
  • Lessons Learned Sessions: Facilitate discussions to extract insights and enhance team readiness.
  • Continuous Improvement: Enhance incident response plans and procedures based on review findings.

Core Infrastructure

  • Water Treatment Facilities

    Critical for ensuring safe and reliable water supply, where disruption can impact public health.

  • Power Generation and Distribution

    Includes renewable energy plants (solar, wind) and traditional power grids, crucial for national security.

  • Nuclear Power Plants

    Extremely high security requirements due to the potential catastrophic impact of disruptions.

  • Natural Gas and Oil Pipelines

    Significant targets, especially with remote monitoring systems that can be vulnerable to attacks.

Industrial Manufacturing and Processing

  • Oil Refineries

    High-stakes environments with complex processes that can be vulnerable to cyber threats.

  • Chemical Processing Plants

    Vulnerable to both physical and cyber threats, as breaches could lead to hazardous releases or compromised production.

  • Pharmaceutical Manufacturing

    Heavy reliance on OT for precision in production and compliance with strict regulations.

  • Food and Beverage Processing

    Uses automated systems that can be disrupted, affecting supply chains and product safety.

  • Mining Operations

    Complex machinery and OT are essential, and any disruption could impact productivity and worker safety.

Technology-Driven Industries

  • Automotive Manufacturing

    High level of automation and robotics requires OT security to maintain production integrity.

  • Aerospace and Defense

    OT cybersecurity is critical due to sensitive data and equipment requiring high security.

  • Agricultural Technology (AgriTech)

    Smart farming systems, automated irrigation, and crop monitoring are growing in automation, requiring OT security.

Healthcare and Building Management

  • Healthcare Facilities

    OT in hospitals, especially in smart building systems (HVAC, lighting) and medical equipment, can impact patient care.

  • Building Management Systems

    Smart buildings rely on OT for energy management, access control, and environmental monitoring.

Transportation and Logistics

  • Transportation and Logistics

    Ports, railways, and airports use OT for operations, from scheduling to cargo management.

  • Public Transportation (Rail Systems, Subways)

    Complex OT used for operations, safety, and scheduling requires secure systems.

General and Specialized Manufacturing

  • Manufacturing (General)

    Automotive, electronics, heavy machinery, etc., all require secure OT environments.

  • Logistics and Warehousing

    Automation and OT in warehousing rely on security to prevent disruption in supply chains.
