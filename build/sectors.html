Core Infrastructure

  • Water Treatment Facilities

    Critical for ensuring safe and reliable water supply, where disruption can impact public health.

  • Power Generation and Distribution

    Includes renewable energy plants (solar, wind) and traditional power grids, crucial for national security.

  • Nuclear Power Plants

    Extremely high security requirements due to the potential catastrophic impact of disruptions.

  • Natural Gas and Oil Pipelines

    Significant targets, especially with remote monitoring systems that can be vulnerable to attacks.

Industrial Manufacturing and Processing

  • Oil Refineries

    High-stakes environments with complex processes that can be vulnerable to cyber threats.

  • Chemical Processing Plants

    Vulnerable to both physical and cyber threats, as breaches could lead to hazardous releases or compromised production.

  • Pharmaceutical Manufacturing

    Heavy reliance on OT for precision in production and compliance with strict regulations.

  • Food and Beverage Processing

    Uses automated systems that can be disrupted, affecting supply chains and product safety.

  • Mining Operations

    Complex machinery and OT are essential, and any disruption could impact productivity and worker safety.

Technology-Driven Industries

  • Automotive Manufacturing

    High level of automation and robotics requires OT security to maintain production integrity.

  • Aerospace and Defense

    OT cybersecurity is critical due to sensitive data and equipment requiring high security.

  • Agricultural Technology (AgriTech)

    Smart farming systems, automated irrigation, and crop monitoring are growing in automation, requiring OT security.

Healthcare and Building Management

  • Healthcare Facilities

    OT in hospitals, especially in smart building systems (HVAC, lighting) and medical equipment, can impact patient care.

  • Building Management Systems

    Smart buildings rely on OT for energy management, access control, and environmental monitoring.

Transportation and Logistics

  • Transportation and Logistics

    Ports, railways, and airports use OT for operations, from scheduling to cargo management.

  • Public Transportation (Rail Systems, Subways)

    Complex OT used for operations, safety, and scheduling requires secure systems.

General and Specialized Manufacturing

  • Manufacturing (General)

    Automotive, electronics, heavy machinery, etc., all require secure OT environments.

  • Logistics and Warehousing

    Automation and OT in warehousing rely on security to prevent disruption in supply chains.
